Aubameyangs tattoos include Dragon Ball Z, giant map of Africa on back and Crespo For Life

 Posted on March 5, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  409 words  |  Larita Shotwell

IT is a growing trend for footballers to plaster their bodies in ink - and Pierre-Emerick Aubameyang is no exception.

Underneath the striker's Arsenal kit lies an inkredible collection of tattoos.

Aubameyang stands out from the crowd wherever he goes - which is hardly surprising considering his fleet of gold supercars.

And his tattoo collection appears to be growing as quickly as his Arsenal goal tally.

The Gabon international celebrated Arsenal's win over Chelsea in January by getting a Dragon Ball Z tattoo. 

He jetted off to Spain to meet up with his regular artist Alejandro Nicolas Bernal before flying back for a match against Manchester United.

The striker scored Arsenal's only goal of the game after he returned with characters Goku and Gohan on his right thigh.

Last summer he decided to get inked up in a bid to occupy himself after Gabon failed to qualify for the World Cup.

The ex-Borussia Dortmund star had two wings coming out the centre of a diamond added to his chiselled torso.


It is a similar tattoo to the one on his back - which pays tribute to his African roots.

He has a giant eagle flying above a map of the country that covers nearly half of his back.

And Aubameyang's dad - who was once rated the best defender in Africa - has explained the meaning behind it.

He said: "I'm sure you'll see him without his shirt on.

"Then you'll see a map of Africa tattooed on his back with a beautiful bird above it.

"I see this eagle lifting Africa, flapping its wings and lifting this map of Africa.

"In his subconscious, maybe Pierre-Emerick sees himself as a player that has to lift Africa."

But his most bizarre tattoo of all pays tribute to his favourite player - former Chelsea star Hernan Crespo.

Sprawled up his left arm in italic font, his tattoo reads: "Crespo for Life."

But it has been reported that Aubameyang's mom is actually related to the Serie A legend.

It has not been confirmed, but the striker has previously sung lyrics about the Argentine icon.

He once said of his idol: "Crespo is dynamic, strong in the air, technically-gifted and a giant of a player."
