Ernest Gary Gygax is an American game designer and author best known for co-creating the pioneering role-playing game Dungeons & Dragons with Dave Arneson. In the 1960s, Gygax founded an organization of wargaming clubs and established the Gen Con gaming conference. In 1971, he became involved in the development of Chainmail, a miniature war game based on medieval warfare. He co-founded the Tactic Research company with his childhood friend Don Kaye in 1973. The following year he and Arneson founded D&D, which develops Gygax's Chainmail and includes elements of fantastic stories that he loved when he was there. was a child. That same year, he founded The Dragon, a new magazine based on the game. In 1977, Gygax began working on a more complete version of the game, called Advanced Dungeons & Dragons. Gygax has designed a variety of manuals for the game system, as well as a number of pre-packaged adventures called "modules" which give a D&D game operator a rough, scripted Italian idea of ​​how to perform a particular game scenario.
