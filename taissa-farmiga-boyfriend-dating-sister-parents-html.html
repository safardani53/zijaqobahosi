She is also well recognized being the sister of the popular Oscar-nominated actress Vera Farmiga.

Sister Vera Inspired To Be Actor

Initially, when Taissa was young, she had no interest in acting but aimed to be an accountant. But she got inspired by her sister Vera Farmiga and made her debut in the 2011 movie Higher Ground. Interestingly, it was Vera’s directorial debut as well.

The film included famous actors such as Joshua Leonard, John Hawkes, Donna Murphy, Norbert Leo Butz, and Bill Irwin. Her portrayal in the movie was quite appreciated despite having no formal training and experience in acting. She then was signed by a talent agency called ICM Partners. Eventually, Taissa took acting as her full-time career.

Taissa Farmiga's Bio: Acting Career That Keeps On Giving

In 2011, she made her TV debut in the horror series American Horror Story: Murder House with the cast including Dylan McDermott.  The first season of the series premiered on 5 October 2011and received wide acclamation.

Not just that, she even appeared in the third and sixth season of the series namely American Horror Story: Coven (2013-2014) and American Horror Story: Roanoke respectively (2016). The movie had enormous success and was even nominated for a total of 230 awards, out of which it won 59.

With a convincing role, Taissa along with her cast earned a nomination for the Online Film & Television Association Awards for Best Ensemble in a drama series in 2012. She was once again nominated for the second time in 2014 for the Best Ensemble in a Motion Picture or Miniseries.

Likewise, in 2015, Taissa got nominated for the Fright Meter Award for Best Actress in a Leading role in the comedy movie The Final Girls. After a year, she made her stage debut as Shelley in the play Buried Child. And in 2017, she became a part of an animated superhero film Teen Titans: The Judas Contract.

As of 2018, she portrayed the role of Emma Ertz in the American drama film What They Had. Her future projects include a comedy film The Long Dumb Road (2018), a horror film The Nun (2018), a mystery thriller We Have Always Lived in the Castle (2018), and another mystery film The Mule (2019).

Taissa's Movie The Nun Opens With The Best Collection Among Conjuring Franchise

Taissa's new movie The Nun, a spinoff of The Conjuring series hit the floors on 7 September 2018. The movie, a fifth installment of the Conjuring saga, had the best opening of all the movies in the Conjuring franchise. 

According to Box office projections, the movie is projected to rake in $45 million in the opening weekend, making it one of the best opening horror movies of all time. 

However, it has not only been making waves in the Box office but has also been a hot topic among critics, with many labeling the movie's climax deadly scary. Taissa's performances also have not gone unnoticed and garnering rave reviews for her portrayal of a novice faith practitioner. 

Taissa Opens Up About Her Depression Battle

The actress who has been making big waves in Hollywood also has been hit by her superstardom. In May 2018, she devoted a post on her Instagram so as to raise awareness about mental health. 

And about three months later, she has finally spoken of her battle with depression and anxiety. Speaking to IANS, she revealed that she had been struggling with mental health issues, and also opened up that she had been battling anxiety for a long time. 

Speaking about her struggle with depression, she had this to say: 

I will be honest... I have been dealing with anxiety for a bit now . And when I first started having it, I would get a little angry at myself because it felt like I was dealing with nervousness and paralysing anxiety for no reason. And that is what I used to get angry about -- the no reason part

However, having now finally got over it, she has been helping raise awareness, against the disease. 

Parents Homeschooled Her To Support Career 

Taissa, who was born on 17 August  1994 to parents Luba and Mykhailo Farmiga, as the youngest of seven siblings.  Her parents are of Ukranian descent settling in America, with her mother a school teacher and father a system analyst.  

She has a close-knit bond with all her family members and once she was into acting, her parents homeschooled her so as to allow her more free time to pursue acting.
