Carroll’s parents parented him along with his siblings. However, there is not much information about his brothers and sisters.

Pete completed his high schooling at Larkspur High School, Redwood, California. After that, he immediately junior college at the College of Marin, where he practiced football during college days.

Similarly, his interest in football grew during his college days. Pete demanded a transfer from Marin. He went to the University of Pacific to study football.

However, after that, he gave up studying for a while and started to play football.

Pete’s massive interest in sports shaped his football career. He worked hard to become a professional footballer. But he didn’t become one. Instead, Pete got invited to work under the coach.

After a couple of years, he joined the Bachelor of Science, a business degree. In 1973, Pete completed it. It was a degree that was unrelated to his career and field.

After two years, Pete also completed his Masters’s degree. He studied for Masters’s in Physical education while working as an assistant for the Pacific.

Pete Carroll | Professional Career

Pete’s professional career started during his college days. His head coach Chester Caddas called him to become a graduate assistant. In 1973, the University of Pacific officially signed him as an assistant coach.

Consequently, he was hired by the University of Arkansas in 1977 as a graduate assistant. However, he was given more duties and responsibilities as an assistant than he did for the Pacific.

In the same way, Pete’s popularity grew stronger during football games. Finally, in 1984, he got hired as defensive backs coach for the Buffalo Bills. Though, during his coaching period, the club did not obtain any titles.

Carroll – Hired, Fired, and again Hired.

Carroll’s career turned when he got appointed as the head coach for the New York Jets. His responsibility for the team was to bring the trophy, which the Jets had never won.

But after several losses, the management fired Pete from the coach’s position.

In 1997, Pete got appointed as the head coach of the New England Patriots. The then coach of the Patriots resigned for some personal reasons.

Carroll found himself in a difficult position because he was a freshly fired coach from the Jets.

He spent two seasons with the Patriots. Pete’s coaching career did not turn successful for the Patriots. After several disappointing losses, Pete got fired from the club in 1998.

Contract with Seattle Seahawks

Pete retired from the Trojans and signed for Seattle Seahawks in 2010. He and the Seahawks drafted a contract for five years.

After the trophy, the club extended his contract, and he remains the coach of the Seahawks, till now. During his coaching, various records got broken.

The Seahawks score defense four years straight. It was the first team to do so after the 1950s Cleveland Browns.

Coaching Style 

Pete Carroll has been an exclusive coach and is also known for his back-to-back Super Bowl wins. Additionally, he is best-known as a “player’s coach,” and is recognized for his unique skills and methods.

One of his widely known techniques is to have fun while being competitive. Right before the games, he blasts huge songs before the game to boost their confidence. Furthermore, he organizes a mini-basketball hoop at their training facility to test players’ focus. 

In the meantime, Carroll also organized a beach volleyball tournament while in the USC. Apparently, it had the sole focus of understanding the line between college parties and games. 

Likewise, he emphasizes gameplay in the action of rugby asking players to use their shoulders rather than their head. Similarly, he always tries to gain new skills for the players and thus, watches YouTube videos for updates.

Moving on, he also indulges in the TV broadcast’s videos. Altogether, he is an aggressive player and often tries to understand a player’s personality. He also believes in constant interaction with his assistant coach and players for better understanding.

Overall, he has also written an autobiography regarding his ethics, philosophies, and goals. 

Controversial Decision

Glancing back in Super Bowl XLIX, Pete Carroll’s decision for the game went viral for all the very negative reasons. Indeed, he faced numerous criticisms right then and there and even got the title as the worst coaching decision ever. 

Well, it was in fact one of the most controversial decisions ever in history that even Emmitt Smith declared it to be the worst call in the history of the sport. Back then, Carroll called for a pass after the last-moment interception instead of a hand-off. 

As a result, they did face a heartbreaking loss in the Super Bowl. As the videos and comments went viral, he was blamed and called out for his decision. Anyways, he tried for the best strategy for his team, which unfortunately didn’t prevail as expected. 

 “We knew we were going to throw the ball once in that sequence, somewhere, and suddenly they had a goal-line defense that we could blow up, so we did, and it just did not work out. It was a hard challenge. It was really hard on some players. And some of us will never get over it.”
