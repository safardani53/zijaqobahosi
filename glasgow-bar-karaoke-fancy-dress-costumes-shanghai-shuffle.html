Inside Glasgow bar with its own fancy dress room full of costumes - even Gerard Butler's a fan

 Posted on August 15, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  463 words  |  Aldo Pusey

THEY say people make Glasgow, but so do pubs - and Scottish punters are always up for a pint served with a side of laughter.

The bustling city has plenty of trendy watering holes but Shanghai Shuffle has something more unique to offer customers - and even Gerard Butler's a fan.

We took a look inside the beloved Glasgow bar famed for its "weird and wonderful" nights, complete with its own fancy dress room and face painting stall.

Owner Tony Tsang said his Chinese restaurant accidentally became a karaoke bar after one booked birthday parts over two decades ago.

Now, it's packed out every weekend - with 100 people already in full singing mode when we arrived last Friday night.

Tony, 61, told the Scottish Sun Online: "We've been doing this for 23 years. The karaoke started when someone hired the restaurant for a party, and they hired in karaoke.

"The people were having so much fun. So every time someone booked the restaurant for a birthday or hen night or something, we asked, would you like karaoke?

"Gradually we said we could do that as a usual thing every Friday and Saturday.

"We have our own DJ, our own equipment set up.

"It just started with one birthday party - that started all this."

The Glasgow local admitted that this job will not make him a millionaire, but he is happy to have a wage and most of all see the smiles on people's faces each night.

He said: "The face paint is free. The costumes are free. We give them hats."

His daughter Wendy Lai, front of house manager, added: "Mum and dad's idea, they just thought it was fun to add dress up as well.

"We buy the outfits from the costume shops in Glasgow, online, wherever we can find nice costumes.

"We used to have a lot of Elvis ones. Now more popular ones are the banana and the chicken, cows as well.

"People love it. A few drinks and then a costume - and then they'll probably regret it the next day."

And their unique perks has even attracted some famous faces.

Actress Kathleen McDermott worked for Tony from the age of 18 until she became a star, and brought Gerard Butler along with her on a visit to sing Brown Eyed Girl.

Tony said: "Gerard Butler is funny. He's an amazing singer."

Wendy added: "Kathleen McDermott has become a family friend. Every time she's in Glasgow she just can't resist us, she loves coming back to sing with us.
