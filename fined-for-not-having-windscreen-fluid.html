Is it illegal to not have any windscreen washer fluid?

 Posted on March 24, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  419 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

IT'S essential that you check your washer fluid levels and make sure you consistently top them up.

Here is what you need to know about whether it's illegal to not have any windscreen washer fluid, rules and fines.

Can I get fined for not having any windscreen washer fluid?

It’s a legal offence to drive with no washer fluid, and you might be fined if you get caught without any.

If you’re in an accident caused by restricted vision and you're caught without washer fluid, sentences might be harsher as it'll be deemed your fault.

You could be charged with careless driving and receive a fine of £100.

From a practical perspective, winter driving can often cause dirt and grit to cover the windscreen, which can restrict your visibility if not cleaned.

Topping up your washer fluid is very simple.

Just open your bonnet and fill the container with screen wash.

It can also be a good idea to add a small amount of de-icer to the liquid, which will help you to deal with ice on the windscreen.

How often should I check my windscreen washer fluid?

It's recommended to check your windscreen fluid at least once a month and top it up if necessary.

But you might need to check more regularly during the winter season or periods of increased driving, depending on how often you use it.

If your windscreen fluid container is less than a quarter empty, make sure to fill it up as you never know when you'll need to use it.

You can top up your washer container with a blend of water and a concentrated screen wash solution to help keep it clean - usually available from any store stocking car accessories.

For colder conditions, you can add antifreeze to the solution to ensure the water doesn't freeze overnight.

Avoid adding detergent to the container as this can leave a soapy layer on your windscreen and is difficult to wipe away.

While you're making sure that your windscreen is all clean to drive, it won't hurt to take a look at your wipers too.

During Winter we expect more rainy days which might hinder our visibility while driving and that is why you need to make sure your wipers are working properly.

Not only because you can cause an accident and hurt yourself and others, but you might end up hurting your bank account too as faulty wipers can land you a fine.
