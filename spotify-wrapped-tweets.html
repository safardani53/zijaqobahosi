Spotify Wrapped 2022 is already popular on Twitter. Here's everything we know about about the annual report as well as funny tweets from listeners.

It's almost here! Spotify Wrapped takes all the music you listened to this past year and throws it in front of your face as if it's not the most embarrassing thing you'll ever see in your entire life. Your annual stats will reveal details like your top five most-played songs and artists, the amount of time you spent listening to music, your favorite genres, and more.

And trust us when we say that Spotify Wrapped tracks everything. Even if you spent most of the year listening to fresh tracks from Lizzo or Jack Harlow, your Spotify Wrapped will also reflect that one weird month you had where you had a rough patch and listened to only the cringiest of cringy (read: the soundtrack from the live-action adaptation of Pinocchio).

So, when can you expect your Spotify Wrapped for 2022? Below, we share details on the release date, as well as some of the funniest tweets from listeners.

Here's when you can expect spotify wrapped to drop in 2022.

On Monday, Nov. 28, Spotify tweeted, "Want to be the first to know when #SpotifyWrapped is here? Like this tweet and we'll remind you!" In other words, it's almost time!

Typically, Spotify Wrapped becomes available within the first week of December. And given that we haven't heard any news from the music platform that says otherwise, we can assume this year will be no different.

That said, we can probably expect our Spotify Wrapped to drop on either Thu. Dec. 1, or Fri. Dec. 2.

As we near closer to that date, listeners are already making predictions over Twitter about what their Spotify Wrapped will reveal about them. Users are also making excuses for some of the embarrassing stats they're anticipating. Check out these tweets!

When does Spotify Wrapped stop tracking?

In an ideal world, our Spotify Wrapped would contain up-to-the-minute information. However, in order to prepare a report for each and every listener, Spotify needs some time. Per USA Today, Spotify only collects data from Jan. 1 to Oct. 31 each year. Therefore, if you go rogue listening to Mariah Carey's "All I Want for Christmas Is You" song every November and December, your secret is safe with Spotify.
