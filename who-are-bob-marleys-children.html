Who are Bob Marley's children?

BOB MARLEY is a Reggae icon, and remains a top-selling artist over thirty years after his death.

Music has also been a bit part of his children's lives, but who are they and what was Bob's net worth when he died?

Who are Bob Marley's children?

Marley had four children with his wife Rita, who he married in Kingston, Jamaica, in 1966.

He also adopted two of her kids from her previous relationships and acknowledges five others from different women.

Although many other individuals have claimed to be his children too over the years, Marley only officially recognised 11.

Four of the Marley siblings even formed the reggae group Ziggy Marley and the Melody Makers on the request of their father after his death.

The group was nominated for seven Grammys and won three of them.

Bob passed away on May 11, 1981.

Due to his fame, Bob was given a state funeral in Jamaica.

It was held on May 21, 1981, and was attended by thousands of fans from all over the world.

Sharon Marley Prendergast

Marley's eldest was born on November 23, 1964.

She is the daughter of Rita, who Bob adopted when the pair married.

Sharon is a singer, dancer and curator and featured in the family group Ziggy Marley and the Melody Makers with her younger siblings.

Cedella Marley

Cedella, Rita and Bob's daughter, was named after her grandmother and was also part of the family group.

She was born on August 23, 1967, and has been a singer, dancer, fashion designer, actress, author and entrepreneur.

She even passed her family's musical talent on to her own sons Skip and Soul-Rebel.

Cedella has been very philanthropic over the years, acting as director of the Bob Marley Foundation to help educational and community development in Jamaica, and becoming a team benefactor to the women's national football team.

David 'Ziggy' Marley

Ziggy, also son to Rita and Bob, was the lead singer of the sibling's group and went on to have solo success with eight solo studio albums.

He was born on October 17, 1968.

Ziggy self-released songs with his own record company Tuff Gong Worldwide, and won eight Grammy Awards and a Daytime Emmy Award.

After his lucrative musical career, Ziggy now writes children's books.

Stephen Marley

The fourth member of the Marley family group was Stephen, also known as Ragamufin, born to Rita and Bob on April 20, 1972.

He is an eight-time Grammy Award winner, three times as part of the Melody Makers, three times as a solo artist, and twice as a producer for his younger brother Damian.

Robbie Marley

Robbie was Bob's son with Pat Williams and not a member of the Melody Makers.

He was born on May 16, 1972 - just a month after his half-brother Stephen.

Robbie has led a quiet life but did appear in a documentary with his older half-siblings to uncover his family's musical roots.

Rohan Marley

Rohan was born just three days after his half brother Robbie on May 19, 1972.

He is the third son of Bob Marley to be born that year.

Rohan, who is the son of Bob and Janet Hunt, is an entrepreneur and former football player.

He himself has five children, including model Selah and footballer Nico.

Karen Marley

Karen was born to Bob and Janet Bowen in 1973.

She took the route into the fashion industry rather than following her musical family and is now a designer with her own label called By Karen Marley.

According to her website, she was born in England but raised in Jamaica, and she shares her father's vision for healthy, happy, and peaceful living.

Stephanie Marley

Stephanie was acknowledged as Bob and Rita's daughter, although her grandmother claimed she was the daughter of a man that Rita had an affair with.

Julian Marley

Julian was born on June 4, 1975.

He is a British Jamaican reggae musician, songwriter, producer and humanitarian.

His mother is Lucy Pounder, but he certainly took after his father as a devout Rastafarian whose spirituality inspires his music.

Ky-Mani Marley

Ky-Mani, whose East African name means adventurous traveller, is the only child of Bob and Anita Belnavis, a Jamaican table tennis champion.

Born on February 26, 1976, he has made a name for himself as a musician and actor, starring in multiple films and recording several albums.

Damian Marley

Damian "Jr Gong" Marley is the reggae legend's youngest.

He was born on July 21, 1978, to Cindy Breakspeare, a former Miss World.

He was only two years old when his father died, but nonetheless, had a notably career as a lyricist, singer and rapper.
