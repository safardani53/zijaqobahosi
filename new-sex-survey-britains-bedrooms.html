New sex survey lifts the covers on what's going on in Britain's bedrooms, including what men really

 Posted on February 4, 2024  |  7 minutes  |  1347 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

A GROUNDBREAKING new survey of 10,000  Brits has revealed that the nation is saucier than ever.

An exclusive survey carried out for Sun Online found that a third of British men fantasise about watching their partner have sex with other guys - known as cuckolding - and 71 per cent of women pleasure themselves at least three times a week.

It also found 84 per cent of millennials are having sex twice a week, while 38 per cent of men are watching porn every day.

Carried out by British Condoms, the country’s largest condom store, the survey asked 10,000 people about their sex lives and the results are sizzling.

84% of millennials have sex twice a week

Despite high levels of porn consumption and self-pleasuring, Britain seems to have kept up its enjoyment of actual sex.

But it's not great news for the over 40s. Almost a quarter either don't do it at all or just once a week, with the largest chunk of this demographic - 62 per cent - having sex between two and three times a week.

Eighty-four per cent of millennials are sexually active at least twice a week but it's the 18 to 21-year-olds who are setting an impressive pace: 89 per cent of them are sexually active at least twice a week, while 22 per cent of them are active five times.

A third of 18-21-year-olds have cheated

The younger generation also appear to be a lot more unfaithful.

Of those in a relationship, only 18 per cent of the over 38s said they had cheated on their partner but a staggering 29 per cent of the youngest group said they’d had sex with someone else — a big jump up from 19 per cent last year.

Millennials (22 to 37-year-olds) have also been naughty with 22 per cent of them — up from 14 per cent in 2018 — saying they’d been unfaithful.

They're also the most promiscuous

A whopping 78 per cent of 18-21 year olds said they’d had six or more sexual partners.

Just 51 per cent of millennials and 61 per cent of over-38 group had similar numbers.

The 18-21 year olds are also having the most sex in public, with 64 per cent of them taking their sex outdoors.

But despite all this sex, the use of 'herbal' performance-enhancing supplements has surged drastically, as men take products like like Virility Max and Golden Root.

In 2015, only 9 per cent of men admitted to using these supplements to maintain a 'stronger' erection. In 2018/2019 that number exploded to 29 per cent.

Men dream of seeing their partner have sex with someone else

For Britain’s men, three is definitely not a crowd when it comes to their saucy imaginations.

Not only does a threesome remain the nation’s favourite sexual fantasy, almost a third of men dream about ‘cuckoldry’ — the act of seeing their partner having sex with someone else.

Maybe it’s voyeurism but a quick straw poll of mates came up with the suggestion that the men who want others to service their wives are just lazy couch potatoes, happy to sit back and munch Pringles while someone else does their marital chores.

Almost 40 per cent of men and women over the age of 38 said cuckoldry was the sexual fantasy they’d most like to try in 2019.

90% of men pleasure themselves four times a week

An astonishing 90 per cent of men admit to masturbating at least four times a week and 22 per cent on a daily basis.

Sisters are doing it for themselves with almost as much enthusiasm: 71 per cent admit to masturbating at least three times a week, while 15 per cent are doing it every day.

These figures show a big rise since 2015, when only 1 per cent of women were daily self-pleasuring, while the overwhelming majority — 67 per cent — were doing it 2-3 times a week.

Blokes have actually tailed off over that same period, dropping down from a daily high of 41 per cent in 2016, and will need to work hard to maintain their slight edge in an increasingly close-fought contest.

Sex toys aren't just for the ladies

Women have traditionally been more willing to use toys to sort themselves out but the new survey reveals that men are getting tech-savvy too.

In 2018, only 24 per cent of them said they had used a sex toy.

This year that number has more than doubled to 58 per cent and their weapon of choice is the 'fleshlight', a torch-shaped toy for men which mimics a vagina.

Women’s use of toys has gone up too, from 74 per cent last year to 79 per cent.

A third of young people caught an STI last year

All age groups have seen an increase in the catching of sexually transmitted diseases.

A disturbing 29 per cent of 18 to 21-year-olds admit they caught something last year — up from 19 per cent last year — while 22 per cent of millennials said they’d contracted one.

Even 11 per cent of those old enough to know better, aged over 38, had also picked up something.

It looks like sex in Britain is getting riskier, with Brits increasingly happy to play Russian roulette with their health, preferring to have casual sex ‘bareback’ — without a condom.

38 per cent of 18 to 21-year-olds said they’d sometimes had casual sex in the past year without insisting on protection.

Millennials played it only slightly safer: 35 per cent sometimes had unprotected casual sex.

The oldest group were the biggest gamblers, with 45 per cent prepared to have casual sex without a condom.

38% of men watch porn every day

Given Britain’s appetite for self-pleasure, it’s no surprise that porn-watching is going through the roof - 38 per cent of men say they watch it every day, while 74 per cent watch at least four times a week.

Women are getting a stronger taste for it too. In 2016 only 2 per cent said they used pornography 4-5 times week. In 2019 that number has jumped to 32 per cent.

The increased use of smartphones and faster internet access have brought pornography to the lives of more Brits — especially women.

Last year, only 3 per cent of women watched porn every day. In 2019 that figure has grown to 16 per cent.

82% disappointed by package size in d*** pics

Brits are happy to flaunt their cheeky bits now more than ever, with a whopping 88 per cent of women saying they have sent someone a naked picture of themselves via text, social media or electronic means.

This is a huge leap in female exhibitionism from 2015, when only 64 per cent of women were prepared to send someone a nude picture.

Men were even less coy, with 83 per cent saying that they had sent a nude shot of themselves.

But while the babes of Blighty are more brazen than ever, they’re not impressed with the state of the nation’s manhood.

When it comes to selfies, size definitely matters: a massive 82 per cent of women were disappointed by the d*** pics they had been sent over the last year.

But with so much porn being watched, it's hardly surprising they find the d*** pics of mere mortals prove a let down after watching the professionals.

Time to try the zoom lens, guys.
