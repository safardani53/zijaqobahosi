Career

Ezekiel Elliott has been dubbed the most complete running back to enter the NFL since Adrian Peterson in 2007. On April 28, 2016, he was also selected by the Dallas Cowboys in the first round with the fourth overall pick in the 2016 NFL Draft. And, leading up to the 2016 NFL season, he was widely regarded as an early favorite for the 2016 Offensive Rookie of the Year while playing behind what many agreed was the NFL’s most dominant offensive line. Then, in May 2016, he signed a four-year rookie contract with the team.

Similarly, after the ninth game of the 2016 season, he became the Cowboys’ second rookie to rush for more than 1,000 yards in a season. He even became the third running back in history to rush for 1,000 yards. He finished his rookie season as the NFL’s leading rusher and the youngest player to reach 1,600 yards.

As a result of his successful season, he was named First-Team All-Pro and qualified for his first Pro Bowl. Elliott was suspended due to allegations that he was abusive to his girlfriend. He decided to accept the suspension and leave the country for a short period of time to train. He then returned in Week 16 against the Seattle Seahawks in a must-win situation, rushing for 97 yards on 24 carries.

In 15 games and starts, Ezekiel Elliott finished the 2018 season with 1,434 rushing yards and six touchdowns, as well as 77 receptions for 567 yards and three touchdowns. He also won the rushing title for the second time in three years.

Then, on September 4, he agreed to terms with the Cowboys on a six-year extension worth $90 million, with $50 million guaranteed, keeping him under contract through the 2026 season. In addition, he finished the 2019 season with 1,357 rushing yards and 12 touchdowns, as well as 54 receptions for 420 yards and two touchdowns in 15 games and starts.

Ezekiel finished the 2020 season with 979 rushing yards and six touchdowns, as well as 52 receptions for 338 yards and two touchdowns. Elliott’s contract was then restructured by the Cowboys on August 27, 2021. To save salary-cap space, he converted $8.6 million of his base salary for the upcoming season into a signing bonus.
