Im a jeweler - you can get a genuine diamond engagement ring 'identical' to one worth $60,000 for

 Posted on April 1, 2024  |  5 minutes  |  948 words  |  Larita Shotwell

ALL that glitters is not necessarily gold – and a jeweler has revealed how savvy shoppers can get a genuine diamond ring that looks identical to one worth $60,000 for five times less.

The jeweler, Dovid Fehler, said the industry pushes the mantra that you "must buy" pricy flawless diamonds instead of ones with imperfections, however minor.

This can lead to people spending $60,000 on a diamond that, to the naked eye, looks identical to one costing $30,000 or even $12,000.

The huge price disparity is because diamonds are tested according to the GIA scale which grades them based on carat weight, cut, color, and clarity, Fehler said.

He explained that "very subtle" differences in the grading can lead to "very dramatic" price differences.

“This means that with a little bit of education, you can save a lot of money,” he said.

“You don't need a flawless diamond. You don't need a diamond with no marks. There's nothing wrong with marks as long as they're clever and subtle. 

“In fact, the right marks help - they bring down the price hugely and people can't see them,” he added.

Fehler said the everyday person would never be able to distinguish between the pricy diamonds and their more affordable alternatives.

“This is why around 90 percent of my clients sit in front of me after I tell them this and are like ‘wow, if I never had met you, I would have bought something vastly more expensive but looks identical,’" Fehler explained.

Even with his expertise, though, he cannot always convince others to save their money.

"Unfortunately not everyone listens. The reality is that many people have a needless obsession with buying a perfect diamond and are willing to pay a fortune to get one,” he said.

To highlight the disparity, Fehler - who has a decade’s experience in the industry and founded Hatton Garden-based firm London Jeweller - recorded a video with three diamonds that are identical when placed on a ring but cost $12,000, $30,000, and $60,000 respectively.

He explained that the certificate is there as guidance and that “every stone is unique and looks unique.”

“Going in and asking for a VS2 diamond (a grading quality) is like going into a car dealership and asking for a white BMW," he said.

You won’t truly know how high quality the ring is by this grading alone.

“That doesn’t really give you an indication of the performance of the car,” Fehler said.

“There are so many different aspects of how it will drive that have nothing to do with the color,” he added.

Diamonds are also unique in that way.

“There are so many different variations that will determine the price of the diamond,” Fehler said.

“You need to physically look at your diamond to determine which stone is best for you," he added.

Certifications will tell customers the size of each imperfection, but these are not anything you can see with your naked eye.

“This explains why there can be such a drastic price difference between seemingly the same diamonds,” Fehler said.

“The answer is that every diamond is unique and the certificate will not give you an indication of how it will look,” he added.

There’s one quality that you can’t replicate in a diamond, however.

“You need to view the diamond and see the color, measurements, and imperfections,” Fehler said.

"And most importantly how is its sparkle? That is the most important thing,” he added.

When it comes to how impressed others will be with your new engagement ring, they won’t note the diamond’s grading.

They’ll just be amazed by that sparkle, the jeweler said.

“Remember, no one cares what quality your diamond is if it doesn’t sparkle,” he added.

HOW TO FIND AFFORDABLE DIAMONDS

Shopping online can be a surefire way to save money while finding the perfect engagement ring.

However, you need to pick a vetted vendor, and always ask to see the diamond’s certificate before you make the final purchase.

James Allen, Blue Nile, and Clean Origin are some of the best sources for diamonds online, typically offering better prices than the traditional mall diamond chain.

If you do find yourself in a chain jewelry store like Kay or Jared, be sure to negotiate for the best deal.

Above all else, make sure you set a budget and stick to it.

WAYS TO MAKE YOUR WEDDING MORE AFFORDABLE

If you really want to save money on your big day, consider opting for a different kind of ring than the stereotypical diamond engagement ring.

You can always create your own traditions because there are beautiful, meaningful rings of all kinds without the high price tag associated with diamonds.

You can also save thousands of dollars by choosing a venue on a Friday or Sunday instead of Saturday.

Venues are generally less in demand on those days, so they will charge accordingly.

Also, aim to shop for your wedding dress at least eight or nine months before the big day in order to avoid rush fees of up to $500.

Make sure to also follow your favorite local bridal salons on social media to see when their sample sales and trunk shows are.

You could earn major savings off both your dress and jewelry this way.

You’ll also be a savings-savvy couple if you keep your guest list tight and choose a newer photographer who charges a lower rate.
