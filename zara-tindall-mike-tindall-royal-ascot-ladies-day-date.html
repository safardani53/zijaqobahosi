Zara Tindall and husband Mike enjoy cute date at Royal Ascot Ladies' Day as they mingle with other V

 Posted on January 31, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  407 words  |  Martina Birk

ROYAL Ascot is living up to its name as Zara Tindall and her husband Mike Tindall stepped out once more to mingle with other VIP guests during Ladies' Day.

The Royal couple led the way for a third day running, as they stepped out arm in arm and looked forward to a further day of watching the nail-biting races alongside the other Ascot star attendees.

The couple, who are proud parents to three children, took time out of their busy schedules to soak up the sun and atmosphere during the third day of the annual Berkshire event.

Zara, 42, wore a pretty cream dress with small, sheer flowers embossed on it. While former rugby player Mike, 44, was suited and booted with a smart top hat and sunglasses.

But Zara and Mike weren't the only ones pleased to be at the races, following in the footsteps of famous faces like Georgia Toffolo.

Good Morning Britain's Charlotte Hawkins and model and actress Sabrina Elba - who is married to Brit actor Idris Elba - were also pictured arriving at the racecourse as day three commenced.

Royal Ascot

As ever, all ticket holders are required to stick to the very strict dress-code, such as dresses being a “modest length” and straps “one inch or greater”. 

But that hasn’t stopped the celebrity guests from turning heads over the past three days.

On day one of the event, former Saturdays singer Una Healy stepped out wearing a bold green and pink hat adorned with a gold horse statue inspired by legendary jockey Frankie Dettori. 

The 41-year-old teamed her elaborate headpiece – which featured Frankie performing his signature flying dismount – with a simple white midi dress.

The hat was commissioned by bookmakers Ladbrokes in celebration of Italian jockey Frankie, who has won the Ascot Gold cup eight times, and made by hand by couture milliner Ilda Di Vico.

Speaking about the quirky but memorable design in honour of Frankie's last ever Ascot, mum-of-two Una said: “Hats off to Ladbrokes and Ilda Di Vico for my beautiful headpiece. 

“Frankie Dettori has given us some many amazing moments throughout his career and I really wanted to pay homage to him ahead of his last ever Royal Ascot and this gorgeous hat certainly does that. 

She added: “It’s a real crowd-pleaser.”
