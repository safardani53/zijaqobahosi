Who are Chris Daughtry's kids?

 Posted on March 15, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  588 words  |  Martina Birk

AMERICAN Idol winner and country music artist Chris Daughtry is the father of three children, one of whom passed away on November 12, 2021.

Daughtry has two children with his wife Deanna and two older children from Deanna’s first marriage.

Does Chris Daughtry have twins?

On November 17, 2010, Chris and Deanna welcomed fraternal twins Adalynn Rose and Noah James into the world.

The twins were conceived via gestational surrogacy, and the couple announced in May 2010 they would be expecting the new additions to their family.

Gestational surrogacy is a newer form of having children when the couple uses the man’s sperm and the woman’s egg and uses in vitro fertilization to create an embryo.

The embryo is then implanted into a surrogate who carries and delivers the child, or in Daughtry’s case, twins.

Daughtry and his wife turned to gestational surrogacy due to a partial hysterectomy Deanna had in 2016, leaving her unable to have more children.

When the couple announced the pregnancy, Daughtry said they were “overjoyed by this double blessing.”

Who is Chris Daughtry’s stepson?

Daughtry is stepfather to 23-year-old Griffin from Deanna’s first marriage. Griffin is two years younger than his older sister Hannah and appears to be very close to his stepfather.

In 2009, Griffin attended the 2009 AMA awards at nine years old alongside Daughtry, and he was featured in Daughtry’s music video for As You Are which was released on August 8, 2021.

Who was Chris Daughtry’s eldest child and stepdaughter Hannah?

Daughtry canceled the next week of his music tour after the death of his stepdaughter Hannah Price on November 12, 2021.

Hannah was 25-years-old when she was found dead in her Nashville home.

On January 19, 2022 The Daughtry family said in a statement shared exclusively with People that Hannah's cause of death was suicide.

The statement confirmed that Price died of suicide while under the influence of narcotics.
