Five discontinued nostalgic sweet snacks we'll never see again - and fans are so upset

 Posted on March 29, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  563 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

SWEET eaters have been disappointed once again as their favorite treats have been pulled from shelves.

Treats may come and go as companies tend to introduce new items only for them to get discontinued after low sales or production issues.

Fans have seen favorites like Butterfinger BBs, Marathon Bars, Cosmic Candy, and more missing from shelves.

Below, we’ve detailed five snacks that fans have had to say goodbye to.

However, fans may be able to find many of these treats on eBay, Amazon, or specialty stores.

1. HERSHEY'S BITES

A chocolate lover's dream, the Hershey's Bites were chocolate-covered dough appropriately filled with chocolate cream.

Introduced in the 2000s, the company described the treat as "soft donut bites with a sweet and creamy chocolatey filling," according to its packaging.

The treats came frozen and just needed 30 seconds in the microwave to give sweet eaters a gooey, chocolatey center.

Each serving was made of four bites with 180 calories and 15 grams of sugar. 

Despite its decadent taste, the bites were discontinued back in 2008.

2. PHILADELPHIA CHEESECAKE SNACK BARS

The Philadelphia Cheesecake Snack Bars were first introduced back in 1999 and made by the Kraft/Philadelphia company, according to the Chicago Tribune.

“Your favorite cheesecake flavors in a clever bar,” the packaging said.

The dessert was easily enjoyed morning, noon, or night as it had a graham cracker crust and a thick layer of cheesecake. 

The cake was available in classic flavors: chocolate raspberry, chocolate chip, and strawberry.

One Twitter user said: “I need the Philadelphia Cheesecake Snack Bars to come back, please I am not a whole person.”

3. DEVIL'S FOOD COOKIE CAKES

SnackWell's Devil’s Food Cookie Cakes were released back in 1992 and were loved by many.

The center had a soft chocolate fudge, coated in a thin layer of marshmallow, all under rich chocolate. 

The company is known for being health conscious which is why it quickly gained popularity during the emergence of diet culture.

Unfortunately, the treat was discontinued after Snackwell's was acquired by Back to Nature Foods Company. 

The company removed tons of artificial ingredients to appeal to a healthier audience, according to the Chicago Tribune.

While it does have a newer version of the treat, it’s definitely not the same.

4. BOSTON CREME ROLLS

The Little Debbie Boston Creme Rolls were individually wrapped dense, yellow cakes.

Each pack came with six ready-to-eat rolls and fans were extremely disappointed to hear they were abruptly discontinued.

One Twitter user wrote: “I'm actually crying about this I'm so upset.”

While the Bostom Creme Rolls are gone, the family-owned company still has its Zebra cakes, honey buns, ice creams, and cereal pies.

5. DEEP FRIED TWINKIES

While this snack has been around for years, the Deep Fried Twinkies by Hostess was popular after county fares fried the treat.

The company partnered with Walmart to distribute the frozen item that was first flash-fried.

The Deep Fried Twinkies could be eaten in under 10 minutes by way of the oven or by air fryer.

However, like most things, it came to an end and fans must now rely on county fares or themselves for the snack.
