Jeopardy! contestant reveals his family suffered a terrible accident before he returns for Tournamen

 Posted on February 14, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  587 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

JEOPARDY! contestant John Focht has revealed that his family suffered a terrible car accident on their way to watch him compete in the legendary game show.

The four-time winner's mother and grandmother came to support him in the Tournament of Champions.

The software professional from El Paso, Texas won four games and $103,800 in Season 37.

He is one of the 21 contestants in the winner's tournament which starts airing on October 31st - it's hosted by Ken Jennings, 48, instead of regular episodes.

The prestigious $250,000 playoffs also features Amy Schenider, Mattea Roach, Matt Amodio, and Ryan Long to name a few - it's already taped but hasn't begun airing.

John wrote to Twitter: "My mother and grandmother were in a car accident, an accident they were only in because they had driven to California to watch me play."

"My mom let me know via text and assured me that they were completely okay and that she just needed to get the car checked to make sure the damage was just cosmetic.

I should not worry and should keep myself in a tournament-ready headspace."

He continued to reveal they made it there, and that he tried his best to keep his game face on - when Jeopardy! came to his and their aide.

'JEOPARDY! CARES'

"I got asked briefly the next day if everyone was okay, but I also saw both my mother and grandmother get asked by different staff and crew throughout the week if they were the ones in the accident, if things had worked out, if they were still good, etc."

"They both felt very cared for (not just for this but also for the planning that let my grandmother get driven around the studio on a golf cart and not go long distances with her cane) and that in turn let me actually stay focused and largely unconcerned."

"All of which is to say, @Jeopardy is full of people who care.

"They care about the show and the people who, even incidentally, make it more of what it is."

'THANK YOU, JOHN'

One Twitter user replied: "That’s so lovely, John. Glad your mom and grandmother are okay."

Another wrote: "A wonderful thread about just how lovely the people who make up the @Jeopardy staff are! Thank you for sharing."

And a third: "I LOVE THIS. Well, not the part that they were in an accident. But, the fact that they were all okay and that Jeopardy! treated them so well and made sure they could come to these events that were important to you."

TOURNAMENT SEASON

Jeopardy!'s brand new season premiered on September 12th and alum Ken is hosting until January.

Jeopardy!'s never-before-done Second Chance competition started on October 17th.

First, two weeks' worth of power players that nearly - but didn’t - win during their original Jeopardy! games last year are duking it out in the redemption tournament.

Then, the victor of each week’s tournament bracket will score the two remaining slots in the Tournament of Champions which airs directly afterward.

When both conclude, regular episodes and reigning ace Cris Pannullo will return in a month.

Both tournaments will be hosted by Ken and will air at the usual time of 8pm on ABC.
