LEWIS HAMILTON may be waiting a while to get his first race win of the F1 season, but his next opportunity will be at the Miami Grand Prix.

Max Verstappen and Charles Leclerc have so far shared two race wins each this season with Hamilton's best finish being third in Bahrain.

It was the Dutchman Verstappen who won out at the Emilia Romagna Grand Prix two weeks ago, as Hamilton finished as low as 13th.

Mercedes have suffered some serious porpoising issues this season but Hamilton seems to be feeling the effect more so than team-mate George Russell.

Russell finished fourth in Italy last month and is also just outside the top three in the drivers' championship standings, four places ahead of Hamilton.

So this weekend's maiden race in Miami looks set to renew Verstappen's rivalry with Leclerc, who is hoping to win his first ever F1 title this season.

When is the F1 Miami Grand Prix?

  • This weekend's F1 Miami Grand Prix will take place on Sunday, May 8 at 8.30pm UK time.
  • The race will take place at the Miami Gardens street track in Miami, Florida.
  • There are set to be around 85,000 people in attendance at the race.
  • Practice One and Two will take place on Friday, with Practice Three and Qualifying scheduled for Saturday,

What channel is it and can it be live streamed?

  • The F1 Miami Grand Prix will be shown live on Sky Sports F1 in the UK.
  • You can live stream the action from the Sky Go or NOW TV apps, both of which are available for download onto your mobile or tablet device.
