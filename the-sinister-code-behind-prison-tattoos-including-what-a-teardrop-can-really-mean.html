The sinister code behind prison tattoos including what a teardrop can REALLY mean

 Posted on July 9, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  707 words  |  Larita Shotwell

FOR the thousands of people locked up in Britain, prison can be a daunting place.

The gangs and cliques make for intimidating neighbours and their alliances are often identifiable by secret codes in their tattoos. Here are the secret meanings behind the toughest symbols that you probably never knew about...

Teardrop

The most commonly recognised of all prison tattoos the teardrop is sported by rappers and celebrities as well as "hot felon" Jeremy Meeks.

Most people believe that a teardrop indicates that the person has committed a murder but it can in fact have a number of meanings depending on your location.

In some places a teardrop means murder, while in others it can represent a long prison stretch or even that your friend was murdered and you intend to seek revenge.

If you ever spot someone with just a the outline of a teardrop - that means they have committed attempted murder.

Cobweb

The tattoo of a cobweb is the sign of someone doing a long stretch behind bars.

The web represents the prison which traps its prey, just as the criminals are caged.

People often sport this on their elbow signifying that they have sat around with their elbows on the table for so long that the spider had time to weave its web.

Clock with no hands

A tattoo of a clock with no hands is another sign that someone is "doing a lot of time".

Some people will have it inked onto their wrist like a watch with straps, while others just have the face of a wall clock or a grandfather clock etched into their skin.

A.C.A.B.

The acronym A.C.A.B. is often integrated into British prison tattoos.

It has two meanings - the most common use is "All Coppers Are Bastards" but some have claimed it means "Always Carry A Bible".

It has been suggested that people get the tattoo to signify the former but when ask claim that they are religious in order to give a better impression.

Playing cards

Particularly popular in Russian prisons - these are the mark of an inmate who likes to gamble.

But more than that, even suits can signify something different.

Clubs symbolise a criminal in general, while spades means a thief.

Diamonds are for informants and often these tattoos have been applied with force.

This is often also the case with hearts, which imply the wearer is actively looking for love in prison.

1488

The number 1488 can also appear separately as 14 or 88 on their own and they represent those involved with Nazis and white supremacists.

14 signifies a 14 word quote by Nazi leader David Lane while 88 signifies the 8th letter of the alphabet twice, HH, which stands for Heil Hitler.

Three dots

Three dots arranged in a triangle shape are the symbol for "my crazy life".

Although it's not associated with a particular gang it represents the criminal lifestyle.

It is often found on the hands or around the eyes.

Five dots

Five dots grouped together is the symbol of jail time in general.

The four outer dots represent the four walls of a prison while the inside dot is a prisoner locked inside them.

This is commonly found on the person's hand between their thumb and forefinger.

Swallow

The swallow tattoo was traditionally a symbol that a sailor had returned home safely after a successful voyage.

But it can also be a symbol that someone has spent time in prison.

The tattoo is supposed to mean that you have "done your bird, done your time".

Where "bird" comes from the cockney rhyming slang "bird lime" which means for "doing time".

Cross on the chest

Having a cross tattooed on to your chest is a sign of a senior gang member.

Those with the inking are referred to as "The Prince of Thieves" - a high gangland accolade.
